Questions leaders actually ask

What devices does it run on?

Any modern web browser, plus a native Android app. On iPhone, open it in Safari and add it to your home screen — it runs like an app, no App Store needed. The whole site is built phone-first either way.

We already run everything on Telegram — do we have to leave it?

No. Keep your group chats for the day-to-day chika. Insure Portal takes over the parts Telegram was never built for — production tracking, approvals, and a training library that doesn't get buried.

Our monitoring is in Excel — do we lose all of it?

No. The company's existing Excel exports import as-is, no reformatting. And the dashboard exports back to Excel any time you need it — it's an output now, not your only copy.

Who approves new accounts?

Managers do. A new signup is held in a pending state and sees nothing — no roster, no feed, no numbers — until the right manager approves it.

Where does our data live?

In a managed cloud database with row-level security. Access rules are enforced at the database itself, so each branch and unit sees only its own data — regardless of how they connect.

Can we import our existing records?

Yes. The company's standard Excel exports — Agent List, Policy Issued, NAP Report — upload as-is, no reformatting. Agents who haven't signed up yet are held in a roster and linked automatically when they register.

What happens when an agent leaves?

Delist them. They disappear from the active roster, but their account and full history stay on record — and if they come back, relisting takes one click.

Who's Kal?

Kal is the assistant built into the app — the shield on the logo, with a face. Ask it about your own production, goals, incentives, or recruitment, and it answers using your real numbers, not a canned response. It only ever sees what you're already allowed to see.

What happens when my trial ends?

Nothing gets deleted. Your account drops to the same free access non-coded agents have — feed, messages, recruitment, and resources — with every bit of your history intact. Upgrade any time and you pick up right where you left off.

Who pays — the agent, or the agency?

Either. Agents can pay for their own plan individually, or a branch or district can put the whole team on one bill. Ask about district-wide billing at the demo.

What's a staff account?

A login for your BOS or office staff with exactly the abilities you give them — like coding new agents — without making them a manager. You pick what each one can do, and everything they do still stays inside your own scope.
